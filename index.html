Title: Tornado Physics Simulation

Summary: In our project, we will be simulating the physics of dust particles in a tornado. We will use semi-transparent dust for realism.

Team Members: Morgan Higginbotham (3034765696), Alex Yen (3034626687), Gabriel Khouri-Haddad (3033759187),  Joshua Chang (3033603226)

Problem Description: We are trying to physically simulate a tornado, as well as create photo realism. This is important because in simulating realistic weather events, tornados play a key role. The difficulties that this problem presents are significant. Such as scaling this project to accommodate a large amount of particles, simulating light through semi transparent dust, and applying forces on each of the particles..

Goals and Deliverables:

Plan: We plan on delivering a rendered and animated gif of a tornado simulation utilizing dust particles. These dust particles will not interact with each other, but will be influenced by an overall force which makes up the tornado. We also hope to introduce lighting to the scene, as a volume of dust is a semi-opaque mass and should allow for interesting interactions with a light source.

Quality and performance could be determined by the amount of particles that can be rendered at a time in a reasonable timeframe, though this is just a vague definition for now. Also it should look like a tornado

Hopes: Real time interaction with the simulation, perhaps altering windspeed or the size of the tornado. Another potential thing to add would be getting large, non-particulate objects to be affected by the tornado

Schedule:

  • Week 1 - Compile project into an empty world of sand particles. No semi-transparent lighting has been implemented.
  • Week 2 - Implement Tornado physics
  • Week 3 - Implement lighting
  • Week 4 - Final optimizations.
